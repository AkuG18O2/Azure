Testimonials

Rahul Shah

FACC IITD has been a constant partner in my journey through the 4 years of campus life. What I love about the club is the plethora of opportunities it gave me to work on stuff we never would have imagined in our lives. On top of that, the collective enthusiasm of the members to try out something new is definitely hard to find elsewhere.

Anshul Damesha

Imagine creating something that will feature in the pictures of dozens of the IITD junta or something that will catch people's eyes on campus even after decades. This is the feeling that FACC gives you. Be it making wall paintings together in insti, doing charcoal in hostel library, or participating in Inter-IIT, it has been an enriching experience. The most memorable of all being the visit to NGMA! Additionally, being in FACC has groomed my imagination and visualization skills while giving me various fun memories to cherish.

Animesh Singh

I still remember painting my way (and my roommate's face!) to being the Best FACC Fresher in first year, to being able to paint the winning Samurai mural with my freshers as the FACC Rep which still wows everyone visiting Satpura to this day. While heading the Creative & Deco team for Rendezvous 19, I got to work with a diligent team and we were able to put up the most extravagant show Rdv has seen in the recent years. In spite of the periodic reality TV worthy FACC poltu drama over the years, I've learnt so much from my talented seniors and made lifelong friendships with my diligent juniors - wonderful people I probably wouldn't have gotten to know so well if it wasn't for FACC. I'll always be grateful to this club for the impact I could create (and leave a souvenir of my time here) in the campus through our team's collaborative projects and the impact this club has made on my fulfilling experience here at IIT!

Ekant

Not just have i been a part and arcel of azure over these beautiful years. Azure was integrated to my life in IIT. I remember when I first came across amazing FACC seniors like Animesh and Manav, who told me about Azure, making my very first instinct to be a part of it. IIT life pyaare seniors aur bakchod fachho ke sath kab nikal gayi, pata hi nahi chala. Somani and Rahul made it much easier for me to get through the tough times. Thery were my constant companions♥️. My fondest memory in IIT was late night inter IIT practices. Unfortunately we couldn't go to inter IIT but in even though online Inter IIT, my team showed the same, rather even more enthusiasm and hum first aaye. I am really grateful to my team who supported me in my decisions to bring new reforms and initiatives.

Aayush somani

FACC and Azure, both have been a defining part of my life at IITD. Having been dazzled by the amazing artworks during the BRCA orientation in my first year, I've been interested to join the club since the beginning, but I wasn't so confident about it as I didn't think that my artistic skills were at par with the artists here. I was delighted to know that I didn't need to be a good artist before coming here, I just needed to have the zeal and motivation to learn it here to be a part of the club. I was able to explore digital art which was really new to me, and I had amazing seniors like Animesh, Vaibhav, Sonal who helped and guided me throughout. My time preparing for inter IIT Roorkee and my tenure as the FACC secy have been some of my best times here. Even though COVID put a halt to many things which I wanted to do as a secy, I still had a lot of fun.
